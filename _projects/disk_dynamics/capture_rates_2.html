General expression for the TC rate

Assume a stellar number density $n_*$ and a number density of BHs $f$ times smaller (for a typical IMF, we expect f~10^-3). Also, assume that the characteristic relative velocity between stars and BHs is $v$. Then the total tidal capture rate

$\Gamma_{\rm BH-MS}=3.2\times 10^{-6} {\rm pc}^{-3} {\rm yr}^{-1} \left(\frac{n_\star}{10^6 {\rm pc}^{-3}}\right)^2 \left(\frac{f}{10^{-3}}\right)$ $\left(\frac{m_{\rm bh}}{10 M_{\odot}}\right)^{4/3} \left(\frac{v}{10 {\rm km\, \, s^{-1}}}\right)^{-1.2}$ $\left(\frac{m_*}{1 M_{\odot}}\right)^{\alpha}$,

where $\alpha$ varies between $\sim$0.2 and 0.5 along the main sequence. Note that the above expression implicitly assumes that the star is less massive than the compact object. For simplicity, we have used the tidal coupling constant for a (ZAMS, Z=0.02) solar mass star in the above equation (though in reality the tidal coupling constant is a function of the stellar mass (as well as the age, metallicity, etc.)

If the star's pericenter is too small the star would be disrupted before it is captured. If we exclude pericenters inside of the nominal tidal radius ($r_t=\left(m_{\rm bh}/m_*\right)^{1/3} r_*$), the rate is reduced by a factor of $\sim$2 for $v=10 \,\,{\rm km\,\, s^{-1}}.$

Now the rate of tidal capture between two ordinary stars is

$\Gamma_{\rm MS-MS}=1.6\times 10^{-4} {\rm pc}^{-3} {\rm yr}^{-1} \left(\frac{n_\star}{10^6 {\rm pc}^{-3}}\right)^2 $ $\left(\frac{v}{10 {\rm km\, \, s^{-1}}}\right)^{-1.2}$ $\left(\frac{m_*}{1 M_{\odot}}\right)^{1+\beta}$,

where $\beta$ varies between 0.5 and 1 along the main sequence. For simplicity we have assumed that all of the stars are equal mass. However, the above rate would include some physical collisions. Next, we disallow physical collisions by excluding pericenters that are less than two stellar radii. For solar mass stars this would reduce the capture rate by a factor of 4.6 for v= 10 km s$^{-1}$

Integrating over a velocity distribution

If the relative velocities are too high then a disruption/collision would occur before the star is captured. In reality the stars and compact objects will have a velocity distribution with a velocity dispersion of $\sigma$ We now consider what happens, when the two objects have a Mawellian velocity distribution with a dispersion $\sigma$.

The rate of captures for a particular relative velocity, v, can be written as

$\Gamma_{\rm BH-MS}=\Gamma_o \left[2.2 \left(\frac{v}{10 {\rm\,km\, s^{-1}}}\right)^{-1.2} - \left(\frac{v}{10 {\rm\,km\, s^{-1}}}\right)^{-1}\right],$

where the second term accounts for stars that would be disrupted instead of captured, and

$\Gamma_o=1.47\times 10^{-6} {\rm pc}^{-3} {\rm yr}^{-1} \left(\frac{n_\star}{10^6 {\rm pc}^{-3}}\right)^2 \left(\frac{f}{10^{-3}}\right) \left(\frac{m_{\rm bh}}{10 M_{\odot}}\right)^{4/3} $ $\left(\frac{m_*}{1 M_{\odot}}\right)^{\alpha}$

There will be a velocity $v_{\rm max}$, above which the star cannot be capture without being disrupted ($v_{\rm max}\approx 230$ km s$^{-1}$ for a solar type star). The rate integrated over the velocity distribution is

$\tilde{\Gamma}_{\rm BH-MS}= \Gamma_o \left[2.2 g(\sigma)-g_2(\sigma)\right]$ (a),

where $g(\sigma)=\frac{2^{2/5} \left[\Gamma \left(\frac{9}{10}\right)-\Gamma \left(\frac{9}{10},\frac{v_{\rm max}^2}{2 \sigma^2}\right)\right]}{\sqrt{\pi }} \left(\frac{\sigma}{10 {\rm km s^{-1}}}\right)^{-1.2} $ and

$g_2(\sigma)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi}} \left(1-e^{\frac{-v_{\rm max}^2}{2\sigma^2}}\right) $ $\left(\frac{\sigma}{10 {\rm \,\,km\,\,s^{-1}}}\right)^{-1}$.

We plot the capture rate from equation (a) as a black line in the plot below. Equation (a) assumes that the maximum pericenter resulting in capture and the relative velcity is a pure power law. In reality, the dependence is more complicated. The dashed red line shows a more precise estimate of the capture rate without this approximation.

We can do the same exercise for main sequence stars capturing each other

$\tilde{\Gamma}_{\rm MS-MS}= \Gamma_o' \left[1.28 g(\sigma)-g_2(\sigma)\right]$

$\Gamma_o'=1.2\times 10^{-4} \left(\frac{n_\star}{10^6 {\rm pc}^{-3}}\right)^2 \left(\frac{m_*}{1 M_{\odot}}\right)^{1+\beta} $

corresponds to the rate at which stars physically collide before they can be captured. Note that we assume equal mass stars for simplicity. For solar type stars, the maximum relative velocity for a capture is $\sim 33$ km s$^{-1}$. $\beta$ is between 0.5 and 0.8 on the main sequence

Simple prescription for capture rates (integrated over a mass function)

The following expressions are a good approximation to the tidal capture rates integrated over a Kroupa mass function between 0.1 and 1 solar masses:

$\Gamma_{\rm BH-MS}=6 \times 10^{-8} \left(\frac{n_\star}{10^6 {\rm pc}^{-3}}\right)^2 \left(\frac{f}{10^{-3}}\right) \left(\frac{m_{\rm bh}}{10 M_{\odot}}\right)^{4/3} \times $

$\begin{cases} \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sigma_o}\right)^{-1.2} & \sigma <\sigma_o\\ \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sigma_o}\right)^{-3} & \sigma \geq\sigma_o \end{cases}$

$\sigma_o\approx 67 {\rm \,km\,s^{-1}}$

(This is approximately a factor of $\sim 3$ smaller than the capture rate for a population of solar mass stars).

Similarly the capture rate between ordinary stars (integrating over a Kroupa mass function between 0.1 and 1 solar masses is)

$\Gamma_{\rm MS-MS}= 1.42\times 10^{-6}\left(\frac{n_\star}{10^6 {\rm pc}^{-3}}\right)^2 {\rm pc^{-3} yr^{-1}} \times$

$\begin{cases} \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sigma_o}\right)^{-1.2} & \sigma <\sigma_o\\ \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sigma_o}\right)^{-3} & \sigma \geq\sigma_o \end{cases}$

$\sigma_o\approx 40 {\rm \,km\,s^{-1}}$

If we instead consider a population of solar mass stars. The capture rate would be

$\Gamma_{\rm MS-MS}= 7.6\times 10^{-5}\left(\frac{n_\star}{10^6 {\rm pc}^{-3}}\right)^2 {\rm pc^{-3} yr^{-1}} \times$

$\begin{cases} \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sigma_o}\right)^{-1.2} & \sigma <\sigma_o\\ \left(\frac{\sigma}{\sigma_o}\right)^{-3} & \sigma \geq\sigma_o \end{cases}$

$\sigma_o\approx 14 {\rm \,km\,s^{-1}}$
